It's time to get serious about online Bitcoin wallets.

To use CarbonWallet you need a passphrase.

We have created a passphrase for you in the box below. Write your passphrase down and keep it safe.

Make sure no-one is near you when you use this passphrase. If anyone sees your passphrase, or gets it from you, they can steal your Bitcoins.

We don't store your Bitcoins locally or on our servers.

Why should I use CarbonWallet ?

We are determined.

CarbonWallet is an online deterministic wallet. Your passphrase is turned into a number which is then turned into Bitcoin keys. Your passphrase is also your backup.

All this happens in your browser not on our servers.

We are a zero footprint wallet.

We don't store your Bitcoins locally or on our servers, only in your computer memory (RAM). No trace is left on your PC once you logout of your session.

Thiefs have fewer angles of attack with CarbonWallet.

We support online or cold storage.

Need to store large sums of Bitcoins ? CarbonWallet supports Cold Storage.

You can use CarbonWallet online or offline on a computer you know is clean and safe. Download CarbonWallet

We will do everything we can to protect your Bitcoins.

However you also need to take responsibility and follow these guidelines.

Never give out your passphrase.

There are several ways thiefs will try and get your passphrase. These attacks apply to all Bitcoin wallets so read up and be safe.

  1. Phishing Always make sure you are on the carbonwallet.com address and never click links in emails.
  2. Social engineering Be very careful about what you install on the computers you own.
  3. Malware including Keyloggers or malicious software often disguised as a genuine piece of software.

Create multiple passphrases.

Think about passphrases the way you keep money in real life, not all in one place. For example.

  • A day to day passphrase. You could store a small amount of Bitcoins here a bit like have a few notes in your wallet or purse. Write the passphrase down and carry it in your wallet.
  • A Bank account style passphrase. One that you only use in a safe environment, i.e. a computer with good virus protection that you are prety confident hasn't malware. Hold moderate amonts of Bitcoins here.
  • A savings passphrase. A a passphrase you created as cold storage. Be very careful when you use this password.

Never lose your passphrases.

Without the passphrase your coins are gone. There is no way in this universe to retrieve CarbonWallet wallets without the passphrase.

The best way to keep a passphrases safe is to do the following.

  • Write them down
  • keep them somewhere safe.

More Technical Details.

We are using 128bit passphrases.

According to "how big is your haystack?" Our passphrases would take 5 hundred trillion trillion trillion trillion trillion trillion trillion trillion centuries to break under a massive cracking scenario.

We are using window.crypto.getRandomValues whenever available for cryptographically random values.

Our passphrases are compatible with the Elektrum Bitcoin client.

We generate bitcoin addresses deterministically.

Your passphrase is turned into a 128bit key which is then stretched into 10 bitcoin addresses. There is no limit but for now 10 per passphrase should be adequate.

Determined Bitcoin addresses mean no need for a remote server to hold keys and no need to store keys on a local hard drive.
